## [cite_start]Artistic Statement [cite: 40]
My story is one of migration. [cite_start]Over the course of my childhood my family and I squeezed our lives into suitcases and moved across three countries. [cite: 41] [cite_start]We left behind our sense of home, our community and our identity and in the process, we had to learn and relearn two languages. [cite: 42] Each move took a huge emotional toll on us. [cite_start]Homesickness kicks in the moment one decides to migrate, and the fear and pain of it and the anxiety triggered by an uncertain future, no matter how meticulously planned, can be overwhelming. [cite: 43] 

[cite_start]WEIGHT LIMIT is inspired by events that took place across the many moves we undertook as a family I reimagined these events in a contemporary Australian context, but the events are as they happened in my memory. [cite: 44] [cite_start]Although most migration stories on film tend to be motivated by catastrophic events like political turmoil (as was the case with Argentina in the late 70s) or economic instability, or the threat of climate change, what I am interested in is the more banal motivation for migration and how it might still have a deep emotional impact, worthy of capturing on screen. [cite: 45] [cite_start]Through this short film, I want to capture the anxiety and grief that comes with migrating, no matter how much you try to embrace the change that's to come. [cite: 46] 

[cite_start]I want this film to be held together by the deep bond of this family, but particularly between the mother and daughter. [cite: 47] [cite_start]Despite the psychological impact this relocation is having on Josefina which is triggering PTSD from her previous move (from Colombia to Australia), the gentle, unwavering love of her daughter and her own resilience and courage (which lives in all migrants) will give her the strength to weather this emotional storm so she can take the necessary leap of faith into a new future. [cite: 48]
