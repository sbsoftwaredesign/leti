# Bean

_A coming-of-age road movie by Leticia Cáceres_

## Logline

When precocious sixteen-year-old Jem discovers she's pregnant in her tiny Australian country town, she convinces her best friend Harry to steal his mum's car and drive her to Sydney for an abortion, but the journey across a vast and unforgiving landscape forces both of them to confront what they really feel, for themselves and for each other.

## Synopsis

Precocious sixteen-year-old **JEM** is determined to escape her small Australian country town. The only daughter of **CHRISTINE**, a single mum and cleaner who fell pregnant as a teen, Jem is terrified of repeating her mother's path. So when she discovers she's pregnant, her world collapses.

Jem hatches a plan. She turns up on best friend **HARRY's** doorstep and convinces him to steal his mum's beat-up 2005 Toyota Tarago and drive her to Sydney for an abortion. They lie to their parents and hit the road, but the journey is anything but easy. The Tarago is barely roadworthy, and they've been forced to bring Christine's beloved pet rabbit, who's sick and needs constant care.

Desperate for cash, Jem calls **BOMBER**, the boarding-school jock she had a summer fling with, and the baby's father. When sparks reignite between them, Harry's jealousy surfaces, revealing feelings he's kept hidden. After the Tarago breaks down for good, Jem and Harry torch it to cover their tracks. In the charged aftermath, they kiss, but instead of bringing them closer, it terrifies Jem. Scared and overwhelmed, she pushes Harry away, convinced she's ruined everything.

When Christine discovers Jem has been lying and travels to Sydney to bring her home, Jem faces her hardest choice: tell her mum the truth and ask for the help she desperately needs, or face the abortion alone.

## Artist Statement

### My Path to Bean

I am a director with over twenty years of experience across theatre, television, and short film, and _Bean_ will be my feature debut.

My career has been shaped by a commitment to stories that centre women, illuminate social fault lines, and demand emotionally truthful performances. On stage, I have directed over thirty professional productions for Australia's leading companies, including Leah Purcell's _The Drover's Wife_ at Belvoir St Theatre, which won the Helpmann Award for Best Direction and Best Production. That collaboration led Leah to invite me as Director's Attachment on her feature film _The Drover's Wife: The Legend of Molly Johnson_, where I gained first-hand experience of the creative and logistical demands of feature filmmaking. A second attachment followed with Michael Rymer on _The Fires_ for ABC.

In television, I have directed across drama, comedy, and children's programming for Stan, SBS, ABC, Network Seven, Nickelodeon, and BBC, earning nominations for AACTA, LOGIE, and ADG Awards. My episodes of SBS's _Erotic Stories_ drew critical praise for their tonal control, balancing emotional vulnerability with humour and sensuality, skills directly transferable to _Bean_.

When screenwriter Claire Phillips sent me her script, I recognised a story I had been waiting for: a coming-of-age romantic comedy with genuine dramatic stakes, a distinctly Australian voice, and a young woman at its centre whose choices carry real consequence. Claire and I partnered with producer Miriam Stein (Tama Films), whose work on _Hey, Hey, It's Esther Blueburger_ signalled a shared sensibility. We have since secured development funding from Screen NSW, and Claire is completing a third-draft polish with the goal of attracting marquee talent and marketplace partners.

### Creative Vision

_Bean_ is a coming-of-age romantic comedy about big dreams, big choices, big distances, and tiny secrets. It follows Jem, a sixteen-year-old determined to escape her small country town, who discovers she is pregnant and convinces her best friend Harry to drive her to Sydney for an abortion, setting in motion a road trip that will test their friendship, ignite unexpected romance, and force them both to grow up.

In classic Australian road-movie tradition, the vast landscape will be a character in the film. I will create a dynamic interplay between vehicle and camera: driving montages that celebrate the thrill of the open road, wide shots that capture the theme of escape, and an evolving visual palette from dry, brittle scrubland to coast and city that mirrors Jem and Harry's transformation. Films like _Thelma & Louise_, _Juno_, and _The Edge of Seventeen_ are touchstones: stories that marry scale with intimacy, and comedy with genuine emotional danger.

But _Bean_ is ultimately a performance-driven film, and this is where my theatre background becomes my greatest asset. Twenty years of directing actors on stage have taught me how to build trust, excavate subtext, and help young performers access complex emotional terrain. When our scenes turn inward (Jem and Harry alone in the car, navigating feelings they cannot name) I will shift to handheld camera with shallow focus, framing them in close, dirty shots that dissolve the exterior world and hold the audience inside the relationship. The goal is a film that feels both visually bold and deeply human.

### Themes and Relevance

Through _Bean_, I want to destigmatise abortion and advance the conversation about reproductive access, particularly for young, rural people. Although abortion is legal in Australia, access remains starkly unequal: geographical distance, cost, and doctors' right to conscientious objection create barriers that disproportionately affect regional communities. The overturning of _Roe v. Wade_ in the US has intensified global scrutiny of reproductive rights, making _Bean's_ subject matter urgent and resonant far beyond Australia.

But _Bean_ will not be a polemic. Its power lies in its warmth, humour, and emotional honesty. I want the audience to fall in love with Jem and Harry, to feel the stakes of their choices not as political abstractions but as lived, visceral experience. I want young viewers to see their own struggles with identity, pressure, and the terror of asking for help reflected on screen, and to feel less alone. Stories like _Bean_ are vital in encouraging open conversation about the complexities of adolescence, reducing stigma, and empowering a generation to face their challenges with courage and authenticity.

### Why TIFF Directors' Lab

_Bean_ is at a pivotal stage: the script is in its third draft, development funding is secured, and a strong creative team is in place. What we need now is the marketplace expertise to take it from development into production. TIFF Directors' Lab's focus on market readiness, combined with mentorship from directors who have navigated their own debuts, is precisely the catalyst this project requires. The opportunity to refine my pitch, connect with international distributors and financiers, and learn from the industry's best would be transformative for both _Bean_ and my career as a first-time feature director.
