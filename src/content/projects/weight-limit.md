# Weight Limit

_A short film by Leticia Cáceres_

## Logline

On the eve of their farewell party, Josefina, the devoted matriarch of an emigrating family, has a shocking change of heart that threatens to derail the family's future.

## Synopsis

_Weight Limit_ is a 13-minute family drama about the hidden emotional toll of migration, even when the move is made by choice.

Josefina, a Colombian Australian computer programmer, has agreed to relocate her family to Japan to advance her husband Carl's career. She embraces the prospect with optimism, especially for her daughter Lucia. But as the family packs up their lives, buried memories of loss and sacrifice from her own migration to Australia resurface, and Josefina swallows her grief to keep up appearances.

At the farewell party, Josefina comes face to face with everything she has built: her community, her career, her closest friendships. The weight of what she stands to lose becomes unbearable, and instinct tells her to run. Her daughter Lucia, terrified of being abandoned, chases after her. In that raw, desperate moment, Josefina finally confronts what she has been too stubborn to admit: she is profoundly afraid of starting over. Lucia, for the first time, grasps the magnitude of this move and the toll it is taking on her mother.

Over ice cream, the two sit together in silence, processing the events of the night. Lucia lets her mother know she is not alone. Josefina accepts her future and recognises that, with the love of her family, she has the capacity to begin again.

## Director's Vision

This is a deeply personal film. Across my childhood, my family and I squeezed our lives into suitcases and moved across three countries. We left behind our sense of home, our community, and our identity. Each move took a huge emotional toll. Homesickness kicks in the moment one decides to migrate, and the anxiety triggered by an uncertain future can be overwhelming, no matter how meticulously planned.

Most migration stories on film are motivated by catastrophic events: political turmoil, economic collapse, climate displacement. What interests me is the more ordinary motivation for migration, and how it can still exact a profound emotional cost worthy of capturing on screen.

I want this film to be held together by the deep bond between mother and daughter. Despite the psychological impact of relocation triggering memories from Josefina's previous move, the gentle, unwavering love of her child and her own resilience will give her the strength to weather the storm and take the necessary leap of faith into a new future.
